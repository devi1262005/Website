Festives,
Joy, happiness, togetherness;
All that it was for her -
She who loved people,
She who loved warmth,
She who did not know it was all played
For the watching eyes, and drawling mouths.


They who acted,
They who laughed with people,
They cuss out in the dark,
They who found no reason to pretend anymore,
For she isn't so little,
And they are too old for the act.


She who spectates the changes,
She who cannot fathom that these
Are the same customs, once followed,
Forced, now thrown and cast away.
She who cannot understand if this saddens
Or surprises her.


They who finally feel free to show their true distaste.
They who find no more reason to hold it all together,
As she watches everything the little girl believed in crumble,
The best parts of the little girl's world,
No longer exist in hers,
There is nothing in this world,
Where she can find traces of the little girl,
Or share the same joy as the child.


That everything the little girl knew was never ever real.
Everything the child knew was wrong,
Perhaps why the child ever so blissfully unaware in her,
Joyous as ever, the child dances and leaps;
As she does not want to kill her joy,
She pretends with the child too,
And takes a bite of the sweets,
Receives 'blessings', those sweet nothings,
With a bow, and a forced smile;
Soundly knowledgeable of the charade.
