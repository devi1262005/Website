Stay, with me,
For me, by me,
The longing plea, unheard,
Whenever someone makes me feel happy and at ease.

With each passing day,
The bond grows stronger,
With the heart like sinking stone,
Drowning in its insecurities and doubts.

The other person,
Who does not deserve to be on the questioning end,
Yet still, my mind ponders,
My heart wavers.

Though it's never them,
That makes me fear being stranded,
It's me, and the struck reminders of the past,
Relationships ruined, staggered.

When I am with them,
Half a mind happy,
Half a mind trying to stay happy.
Half a heart full of life,
Other half full of doubts,
Bracing itself for the days to come,
Where they might not be present.

People do not stay,
But the emotion does,
People leave,
The days of joy, happiness, sadness,
Those days are mine, were a part of my life,
Routines change, but they still are a part of a routine.

Once greet them, now avoid them,
Past stays, and it's a painful reminder,
Of what could have been,
What was lost.

But the people now in the present,
Do not deserve to be viewed with suspicion,
I hate the way I perceive my current people,
With a marred lens of the past.

I cannot really help it,
After all, I am still the little girl,
That spent countless nights,
Observing the raise and fall of my parents' chest,
In their sleep,
In fear they might leave me, for God.

I, though, realize,
I cannot make anyone stay, if they do not want to,
Or can make anyone I cherish leave, because of my fears,
And that death, the inevitable, cannot be avoided.

Is it wrong to need a rock,
In the middle of the crashing waves?
The rock that just stays firm,
Amidst the chaos of waves?
Could I be accepted without a camouflage?
Would anyone stay?

Does the rock deserve the chaos?
And the overwhelming clashing of the waves?
It doesn't,
So it crumbles,
Unregrettably the waves still crash,
Against parts of itself, more intensely,
With nothing to ground,
The rock no longer present.

The fear of being abandoned,
The crippling weight of doubts,
Never truly happy, always bracing,
This is tiring, and I do not know how to stop it.
