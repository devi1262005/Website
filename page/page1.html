As I walk down the street,
My eyes wander searching for the pair of familiar eyes,
That would be shining with warmth, directed my way.
With a thumping heart and twiddling fingers,
I walk towards her.

The moment I reach her,
And the familiar tone of her voice reflecting kindness,
The kindness, that her eyes speak louder,
With me greeting her back shyly, with watery eyes, every time.
The beat of my heart becomes louder;
Than the gratitude I could ever muster up to show her.

Each day she gifts me,
Something from her shop,
Which I decline saying I do not deserve for I do not pay,
She disagrees with one of her brightest smiles,
Which I return with my cheek hurting.
Somedays, it's just one of her smiles,
That I gladly beam back, lost energy back with full force,
These interactions that will forever make my day,
I'm revived after getting drained by university.

This brief moment with her,
Always making me realize I'm loved,
I'm loved as I ever can be,
The kind of love that's so reliving,
Because it comes from a place where I did not prove myself.

That I too am capable of loving,
In a different way, than I'm being showed love,
That I do not have to strive to reciprocate the same way,
Even if I reciprocate her kindness with a red-tinted cheek and a bright smile,
Without giving anything back for what she gives me,
She would always say "I like you".
