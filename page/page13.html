The four walls,
The chairs,
The television,
The radio,
That aged with me.


The four walls that absorbed my laughter,
Saw me in good and bad;
The radio that hid my cries;
The television that diverted the pain;
The chairs I kicked at, sat in;
Everything in my house
That saw me more than anyone did.


The bed I lied in;
The no longer soft bed;
That I lied awake and slept well in;
The bed I shared the daydreams and nightmares with;
The bed that was warm and cold for me;
With fuzzy dreams, happy thoughts;
With fever dreams, gloomy thoughts;
The bed that I had always ended my day with.


I lied awake with rose-tinted cheeks;
I lied awake crying about unfairness;
I lied awake and soaked the bed with tears;
I lied awake talking to objects;
Whose silence bode me well than people;
I am always well accompanied.


All the objects,
The object of my emotions;
The serenity of never-changing objects,
In the ever-changing circumstances,
Should I thank my parents for not replacing them?
Or should I worry about them collapsing?
Seems as though they too are overwrought as I.
