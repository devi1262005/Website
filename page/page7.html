A tender moment lived,
Is a moment stored,
In every place,
In every face.


A moment passing,
Becomes fleeting,
Becomes longing,
Hoping to touch,
Longing to clutch.


An impossible feat,
Achieved by a passing stranger;
Stranger who lives their present;
Somehow strikingly similar to the watcher's past;
Blend of blissful unawareness;
The watched and the watcher.


Both live the same life,
For a brief splitting moment;
Till the present pulls the watcher,
Out of their trance.


The watcher;
Desperate to find similarities,
The urge to predict;
Trying to make it easy for the watched;
Hoping it's the same as how it was;
Gushing out words;
Feigning enjoyment;
While it was a little less felt.
Feigning familiarity;
When all they felt were lost.


The innate desire,
To live the spent moment time again,
With increasing desire each time;
To spectate the same through another,
The joy of familiarity and assumption,
Takes away the pain of uncertain.
