Forgiveness, the greatest gift,
Art of forgiveness, gift bestowed by the creator,
Everyone says.


Forgiveness is selfish,
It frees oneself,
Of the anger, grief, the pain of betrayal,
It is not for the other person to relish on.


You cannot swallow back words spat,
Actions done,
A mere "I would be a better person,"
Wouldn't take it all,
For the better person would not be in my life, completely,
Rather someone else's.


Forgiveness wouldn't result in lost trust gained,
The days cried, months wallowed in self-pity,
Months and days of questioning choices,
Overthinking, where it all went wrong,
The days won't be made better, or brought back.


But rather, forgiveness, itself is beautiful because,
It shows the triedness of the weeping heart,
The heart that is done weeping ready to move on,
Not erasing the pain, or the good memories made,
Or treating the person like they did,
But rather, not let it impact anymore than it did.


People with empty apologies,
Seeking grievances, for their shortcomings,
Trying to make you forgive,
Putting on a show of a dog with a tail between its legs,
Laughed at on the inside,
Forgiven loosely,
And pushed away as they should be.


Forgiving people, who did not even ask for it,
Or have any idea of their actions,
Is even more difficult,
But the heart has decided to let go,
And it shall.
