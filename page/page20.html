Oh Hamster on the wheel,
Do you feel excitement?
Do you feel dread?
Have you ever imagined jumping?
Had you ever imagined not spinning?
Are you a spectator of your own misery too?


Creature on the wheel,
Do you know of the people you spin for?
Had you chosen to spin?
For every time you spin,
Had you imagined a tireless existence?
Do you realize your helplessness being spectated?
Had you imagined a world beyond your control?


Oh Hamster,
Every day you run,
Every day you chase the circle,
Every day you hope for an end,
And every day, every time,
For all the every's,
Hidden is a never.


You shall never find your end,
You shall spin every day,
The end, you desperately,
Toil for, hurt for.
Do you find solace in the predictability?
Hamster oh hamster?
Whatever reason were you chosen for,
You made peace with your fate.


Though,
Don't you wish,
You could stop instead,
To leap instead,
And not chase the never-ending end?


I somehow find myself in your wheels too,
Every phase of mine,
A new wheel,
The old one discarded,
As a fond memory to recall,
For my spectators.


As I spin endlessly in those new wheels,
Hoping to be tire of them,
A new one shapes its way in,
I wish I wasn't the one for the wheels,
I wish I was for the life of uncertainty,
Right and wrong, wrong or right.
I do not wish for everything to be known,
I do not wish to sigh or resign,
And watch it all unfold itself,
A new play, old title.
Playing the lead, or the seer.


I wish the wheels for my feet,
That my paths shall never intertwine,
Every dawn, every dusk,
There shall be only one of its kind,
A whirlwind of endless running, tumbling,
Toppling, nothing shall be laid out.
I hope for a dawn where I know not.


The predictability of my predicament,
Is never-ending,
And it's so wrenching, hamster,
How could they let you do it for laughs?
