The eye sees it all,
Of the lovers in bush,
The lovers that hush,
The lovers from crush,
The lovers that rush.


The lovers who are unafraid,
Finding joy in being known,
The lovers that find strength to fight,
The lovers that find strength to hide.


The lovers together for sake,
Conceiving a child at everyone's wake,
The lovers that learn to love,
As they call it fate.


The lovers whose love might kill,
The lovers whose love is a known tragedy,
The lovers that indulge in love,
The lovers who rewrite their fate, holding hands.


The lovers who part,
Still in disbelief of their depart,
The lovers who let go,
The lovers who immortalize.


The lovers whose loved
Had found another,
While the lover woefully
Resign to memories.


The lovers who infuse themselves
Within their loved,
The lovers known by their loved,
The lovers that learn to love,
The lovers that grow with their love.


The lovers hurt by love,
The lovers misfit,
The lovers declared misfit,
The lovers forcibly part,
The lovers who love,
While tethered to other in name.


The lovers that stay,
With the love that harms,
The lovers that take the love,
And blind to the hurt.


The lovers ashamed to love,
They who shatter their loved,
They who cage their loved,
And repent as they flee.


The lovers as
Providers and receivers,
The providers who feel full,
At their receivers' joy,
Assuring each other of their love.


The unpaired,
Afraid of love.
The unrequited love,
Whose love in vain,
Whose sentiment pains.


The wandering eye sees it all,
As it trails about in its vicinity,
It smiles at every one of the pair,
The eye that makes the heart wonder,
What kind of lover would I be?
