As I look up, I see the broad sky,
The sky that never crumbles,
The sky that always finds its way to shine light,
Amidst the clouds.



The sky that, despite shrouded by darkness,
To rain, as one would call it crying and grieving,
The very sky which shows me that crying too
Can sometimes make your heart feel light and sound,
Just as the rain makes the plants and the people rejoice with joy,
The heart too will be thankful for the burden released with sound,
For it does not feel alone in its pain validated by its beholder.



Just like how after the roaring of thunder,
The light follows.
The grieving heart follows the light of the consoling words
Offered by its companions,
That companionship though can only be achieved,
If the heart can roar like thunder,
Unafraid to ask for help,
Isn't that humorous?



The pitch-dark night sky shows its stars hung up beautifully,
Which most people would sleep on,
The ones that stay hidden to the eye in the morning,
Isn't that synchronous with the saying "you shine when it's your time"?
Or is that synchronous with the saying "beauty lies in the eye of the beholder"?



Perhaps people only stay with you during times of joy,
The splendid bright sky,
They never stay with you during your darkest,
That they never get to see those unhidden parts of you,
The stars that so beautifully ignite with undying courage and determination,
Even though you hardly feel it some days,
Like a dimming star,
It serves as a reminder of what was,
And makes room for what will be.



The stars concealed by the brightness of the daylight,
The stars that only show up during the darkness of the night,
These contrasts are too what makes you, you.
Just like how some places face the darkness of the moon,
And the brightness of the sun,
In the same planet at the same time,
People do too,
Some see you glowing in the face of dark,
Some see you glowing in the face of light,
Based on where you hold them to your heart.
And that is alright.
