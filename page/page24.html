What use of my life,
and its time,
If I do not have any means,
to lay and gaze at the stars?


Whatever joy do I have,
If I cannot spectate the stars?
What curiosity it is,
If I do not marvel at the sky?
Am I really gushing in joy,
If I do not astound at the vastness?


Is it really my home
If I must crane my neck,
Travel miles for minutes,
to glimpse my joyous sight?


I miss my once home,
Where I could,
Lay my bones to rest,
And co-exist with the stars.


I relentlessly toil to behold,
the sight that is mine.
A home where I can lay my body,
Indulge myself in the darkness,
Of the night sky,
Ignoring the weight of self.
